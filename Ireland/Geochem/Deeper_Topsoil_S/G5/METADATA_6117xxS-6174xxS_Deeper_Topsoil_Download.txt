Title		Deeper Topsoil Geochemistry North Midlands Ireland (G5) ITM 

Purpose		
The Tellus project is a survey of heavy metals, organic carbon and pH in topsoils in Ireland. Topsoil is the upper surface layer of the soil and survey measures the amount and type of chemicals in these surface soils. These chemicals can be natural or man-made. This allows us to map different soil types and learn about how they were made.
Deeper topsoil samples were taken from the north midlands (G5), and tested for their chemical make-up. Deeper topsoil is worth testing as it is more related to deeper rock formations than shallow topsoil.
This data will help in land-use planning, mapping of natural resources, help understand impact of farming on the environment and aid policy. Higher amounts of some chemicals in soils may also affect human and livestock health. 

Version		1.1

Summary		
Soil samples were collected in block G5 (Irish North Midlands) between 2017 and 2019 and tested for the chemicals that make up the soil. Over 1000 samples were taken from areas such as meadows, fields, parks and pastures. The samples were taken using a hand auger from depth up to 0.50 m and were collected into plastic bags. The <2 mm fraction of the sample was sent to a lab to be tested for chemicals and the results were given as mg/kg (milligram per kilogram) or % (percent). Data are censored where below Lower Limit of Detection (LLD). For elements where large proportion of observations fall below the accredited LLD, non-accredited, uncensored below-LLD data are also provided (Au, B, Ta, Pd and Pt).
It is a vector dataset. Vector data portray the world using points, lines, and polygons (areas). This soil geochemistry data is shown as points. Each point gives information about where the sample was collected, the type and value of the chemicals that were tested. This includes:
